The Good Book

A practice in gratitude and the awareness of the infinite.

  • The residual heat from a freshly-ironed shirt in winter.
  • Not a cloud in the sky.
  • Sun on legs.
  • A song that fits like a puzzle piece into your current emotional state.
  • A book you just can't stop reading.
  • A story that makes your cry.
  • A dog looking for a pat.
  • Abatement of anxiety, even if only for a short while.
  • Rubbing sleep from your eyes.
  • A cool swim on a hot day.
  • Infectious laughter.
  • A well-earned, perspiring schooner of beer.
  • Fingers running through hair and nails scratching over scalp.
  • A flower dancing in the breeze.
  • Endorphins in the aftermath of a hard run.
  • Riding a skateboard over fresh, smooth asphalt.
  • Gulping down a glass of water when parched.
  • A lawnmower finally stopping on a Saturday afternoon.
  • Warm tea, with steam rising.
  • The penny-drop of understanding.
  • Seeing someone you love get excited and passionate about something.
  • The roar of close to 100,000 at the MCG.
  • A memory on a glossy photograph.
  • Sliding into fresh, clean sheets.
  • Electric blanket in winter.
  • Waking up knowing you've got bugger all to do today, and that's just fine.
  • Tiredness washed away by steaming coffee.
  • A song finishing on the radio the moment you arrive home.
  • The scent of buttered raisin toast in the late morning.
